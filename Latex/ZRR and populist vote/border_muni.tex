\subsection{Border municipalities}

As a corroborative exercise, we restrict the sample to communes sharing a border across the initial ZRR frontier. The current border-pair object contains 13,646 commune-pair observations, representing 6,378 unique communes and 7,412 unique border pairs. Restricting to pairs whose two communes are in the same department yields 9,710 commune-pair observations, 4,813 communes, and 5,236 pairs. These are not interchangeable quantities: a commune can appear in more than one pair. The identifying assumption is consequently demanding: conditional on observed characteristics and the chosen fixed effects, adjacent treated and untreated communes would have had similar FN vote shares absent treatment. We use pair fixed effects to absorb pair-specific level differences, but do not treat this exercise as a replacement for the primary first-difference design.

Table \ref{tab:ttest-border} reports residual balance checks for the border sample. Several covariates remain different across treatment status, so these comparisons require caution. Table \ref{tab:border-results} nevertheless reports negative associations between initial ZRR status and the 2002 FN vote share across its specifications, from -0.0064 in the unadjusted model to -0.0041 with pair fixed effects. The placebo outcome in 1988 is small and statistically indistinguishable from zero. The sign is consistent with the primary first-difference evidence, but residual imbalance means the border estimates should be read as supporting rather than decisive evidence.
