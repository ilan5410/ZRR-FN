\subsection{Heterogeneity}

The average effects presented above may mask heterogeneous responses to the program across types of municipalities. In Appendix \ref{app:hetero}, we explore treatment effect heterogeneity using four methods (OLS with interaction terms, post-selection Lasso, causal trees, and causal forests) and compare the municipalities in the top and bottom deciles of the estimated conditional average treatment effects. The two groups differ mainly in their employment rate, educational composition, share of vacant housing, and locality size, but no single characteristic emerges as a clear and robust moderator of the treatment effect. We therefore treat the heterogeneity analysis as exploratory and relegate it to the appendix.
